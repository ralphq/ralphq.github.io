Penn State Air Vehicle Intelligence & Autonomy Lab

As a concept demonstration and lab component for use in advanced control systems classes, I developed a PID controlled momentum wheel to stabilize an inverted pendulum (example shown below). The pendulum root is not actuated, but by spinning the wheel at the tip into the direction of the fall, angular momentum brings the wheel back to the equilibrium position. This project taught me tons about dynamics modeling & transfer functions, PID tuning for pole placement, and use of microcontrollers for control systems.
