Project Manager & Systems Engineer

Oasis: 2020 NASA BIG Idea Challenge Finalist

Working in the Student Space Programs Laboratory (SSPL), myself and 19 other students were tasked with the development of a Laser Induced Breakdown Spectroscopy (LIBS) system for use at the Moon’s southern pole. LIBS works by exciting electrons in a sample and detected the wavelengths of photons emitted by the sample as said electrons return to normal energy levels; it is a reliable spectroscopy technique previously used commercially and for space exploration, the most famous example being ChemCam, which flew on NASA’s Curiosity rover. The project, named Oasis since it was searching for water, received roughly $150,000 of US funding for the development & integration of the following technologies:

  • Custom optics for laser focusing & spectra collection
  • Flight computer & PCB for data transmission & power distribution
  • Thermal control system for component temperature regulation
  • Vibration-withstanding payload housing & mount

As the project manager & systems engineer for the project, I learned so much in that project not only about the design, manufacturing, and verification of complex aerospace systems, but also leadership, organization, public speaking, and working with others.
