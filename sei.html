Software Engineer for Advanced Computing (AI Division)

Carnegie Mellon Software Engineering Institute (SEI)

Currently I'm a full-time researcher in the SEI AI Division's Advanced Computing Lab (ACL). The ACL combines novel hardware (FPGAs, quantum computers, analog computers, etc) with AI technologies to solve national security problems. My work focuses the implementation of machine learning algorithms on extremely low size, weight, and power (low-SWaP) platforms, often referred to as edge computing. The ability to run computer vision, signal intelligence, or even large language models 'at the edge' rather than on a distant server can increase these models' speed, and security in mission-critical environments.

I actually started this position as a part-time intern in college and loved it so much I came back full-time. As state-of-the-art AI techniques become more and more resource intensive hardware will become a more and more important factor. Many believe this process has already begun, with the 'death of Moore's Law' leading to more research in specialized and heterogenous computing. I'm incredibly grateful for a job that not only allows me to study and implement the latest in AI, but also allows me to peer into the future of computer hardware.
